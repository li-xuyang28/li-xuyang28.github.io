I am a PhD student in Biomedical Engineering at Johns Hopkins University. I earned my undergraduate degree from Northeastern University, double majoring in mathematics and the combined major of data science and biochemistry with a minor in physics. I am broadly interested in real algebraic geometry and its application, namely algebraic statistics (i.e. algebraic representation of graphical models). I am also interested in computational biology, such as analysis of scRNA/TCR/BCR/ATAC/...seq, with an emphasis on immunology. I am particularly interested in causal inference and discovery with help of prior knowledge. My honors undergraduate thesis in CS focuses on applying methods from commutative algebra in score based causal structure learning, and my honors undergraduate thesis in math focuses on algebraic modelings of DNA sequences and DNA motifs, by studying the polytope of word distributions.

My CS thesis work is supervised by Prof. Olga Vitek, and I closely work with Sara Taheri. An important mentor to me in mathematics, and academics in general, is Prof. Anthony Iarrobino; I organized a virtual reading group during the pandemic in algebraic statistics with his help. My math thesis work is supervised by Prof. Harm Derksen, with Profs. Iarrobino and Calina Copos on the committee.
